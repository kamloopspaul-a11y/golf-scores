1. The New "Pro-Schema" Layout for Google Sheets
We are moving away from the traditional "Horizontal" scorecard layout to a "Long-Form" Vertical Schema. This is the secret to high-level analytics.

Structure: Each row represents one hole, not one round. A single 18-hole round will now occupy 18 rows.

The Benefits:

Granular Analysis: Allows for hole-by-hole diagnostics (e.g., "Why do I always double-bogey Hole 6?").

AI Compatibility: Gemini and Claude can "read" vertical data much more reliably to spot trends.

Scale: You can track 20 years of data in one sheet without hitting column limits.

Key Columns: Round_ID, Date, Hole_Number, Par, Score, Putts, FIR (Hit/Miss-L/Miss-R), GIR (True/False), Up_and_Down, Penalties, Stroke_Index, and Net_Score.

2. Files to be Modified
To implement this, three core components of your PWA need an update:

course_seed.json: Update this to include the stroke_index (1-18 difficulty) for all 18 holes. This allows the app to calculate the Net Score on the fly. (See the Mt. Paul update below).

The POST Function: Your JavaScript needs to be adjusted to send an array of 18 hole objects rather than one single summary object.

Google Apps Script (code.gs): The script bound to your Google Sheet must be updated to iterate through those 18 objects and append them as individual rows.

3. Free Plugins & Connectors for Visuals
For the "Contemplative" sessions and clubhouse "WOW" factor, these tools are the best free options:

Connectors (The Data Bridge)
Google Sheets API: The native way to pull your data into the PWA.

Google Looker Studio: Best for your laptop. Connect it to your Sheet for a "PGA Dashboard" look with zero coding.

Visualization Libraries (The Eye Candy)
Chart.js: The #1 choice for PWAs. Lightweight and looks amazing on iPhones.

Google Charts: Specifically for the "Gauge Chart"—perfect for a "Putt-O-Meter" that looks like a car speedometer.

ApexCharts: Great for "Sparklines" (miniature trend lines) that show if Player's score is trending up or down over his last 5 rounds.
