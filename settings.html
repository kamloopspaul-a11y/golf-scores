Tracking Your Stats
We suggest logging at least five rounds — golf has too much variance for a small sample to be reliable. Ten rounds is better. Twenty gives you real signal.

Knowing where you should be is just as important as knowing where you are. Here are realistic benchmarks for each of the key stats (% = avg/round) by handicap range:
HANDICAP FIR % GIR % PEN
0–5 (Scratch) 65–75%55–65%≤0.5
6–10 (Mid-Low) 55–65%40–55%0.5–1
11–18 (Mid) 40–55%25–40%1–2
19–25 (Mid-High) 30–45%12–25%2–3
26+ (High) <35%<15%3+
HANDICAP UD % X-UD % PUTTS
0–5 (Scratch) 55–65%35–45%28–30
6–10 (Mid-Low) 40–55%45–60%30–32
11–18 (Mid) 25–40%60–75%32–35
19–25 (Mid-High) 15–28%72–85%34–37
26+ (High) <20%>80%>36
If any one of your numbers falls significantly below the benchmark for your handicap range, that's where your practice attention should go. If multiple numbers are off, prioritize the one that represents the most strokes lost — generally, GIR and penalty strokes tend to have the largest impact at the amateur level.
