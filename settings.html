Tracking Your Stats
We suggest logging at least five rounds — golf has too much variance for a small sample to be reliable. Ten rounds is better. Twenty gives you real signal.

If any one of your numbers falls significantly below the benchmark for your handicap range, that's where your practice attention should go. If multiple numbers are off, prioritize the one that represents the most strokes gained — generally, GIR and penalty strokes tend to have the largest impact at the amateur level.

GPI is a simplified version that estimates Strokes Gained in a round across four areas of the game — ball striking, short game, putting, and penalties using stats you track.
More ›
Each area is weighted to reflect its real cost at your handicap level. Add them together and you get a single number: your Golf Performance Index (GPI). The lower, the better.
