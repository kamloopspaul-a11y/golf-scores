Golf Stats Tracking – Google Sheets Setup

Sheet Layout (Row 1 Headers)

A: Date
B: Course
C: Score
D: FIR
E: GIR
F: UD
G: Putts
H: Penalties

Calculated Fields:

I: Ball Striking Gap
J: Short Game Opportunities
K: Missed Opportunities
L: Short Game Efficiency
M: Putts per GIR
N: Diagnostic Score

O: Ball Striking Cost
P: Short Game Cost
Q: Putting Cost
R: Total Strokes Lost

---

Row 2 Formulas (Copy Down for All Rounds)

Ball Striking Gap (I2)
=18 - E2

Short Game Opportunities (J2)
=18 - E2

Missed Opportunities (K2)
=(18 - E2) - F2

Short Game Efficiency (L2)
=IFERROR(F2 / (18 - E2), 0)

Putts per GIR (M2)
=IFERROR(G2 / E2, 0)

Diagnostic Score (N2)
=(18 - E2) + ((18 - E2) - F2) + G2

---

Strokes Lost Model

Ball Striking Cost (O2)
=(18 - E2) * 0.5

Short Game Cost (P2)
=((18 - E2) - F2) * 0.7

Putting Cost (Q2)
=G2 - 36

Total Strokes Lost (R2)
=O2 + P2 + Q2

---

Weekly Summary Section (Example: Last 5 Rounds in Rows 2–6)

Rounds Played
=COUNTA(A2:A6)

Average Ball Striking Gap
=AVERAGE(I2:I6)

Average Missed Opportunities
=AVERAGE(K2:K6)

Average Short Game Efficiency
=AVERAGE(L2:L6)

Average Putts per GIR
=AVERAGE(M2:M6)

Average Diagnostic Score
=AVERAGE(N2:N6)

---

Strokes Lost Averages

Average Ball Striking Cost
=AVERAGE(O2:O6)

Average Short Game Cost
=AVERAGE(P2:P6)

Average Putting Cost
=AVERAGE(Q2:Q6)

Average Total Strokes Lost
=AVERAGE(R2:R6)

---

How to Interpret

Ball Striking Cost (Column O)
Higher values indicate missed greens are costing strokes. Focus on iron play.

Short Game Cost (Column P)
High values mean failed up-and-downs. Focus on chipping and pitching.

Putting Cost (Column Q)
Above zero indicates lost strokes on the greens. Below zero means strong putting.

Total Strokes Lost (Column R)
This is your overall performance indicator. Lower is better.

---

Optional Improvement

You can add Penalty Cost by modifying Total Strokes Lost:

Replace R2 with:
=O2 + P2 + Q2 + H2

This includes penalties directly in your total.

---

Purpose

This sheet provides:

- One consistent system for every round
- Clear identification of stroke loss by category
- A way to track improvement over time
- Data-driven practice focus
